Neil Boyce

Neil trained as an electrical engineer at the Norfolk Collage of Arts and Technology (Kings Lynn) and is now the managing Director of an Oundle-based electrical engineering company. Neil also spent six years in the Territorial Army (Royal Electrical and Mechanical Engineers) achieving the rank of corporal. He moved to Castor in 2002 and after leaving the Territorial Army joined Castor Parish Council, subsequently becoming Chairman - a position which he still holds. Neil has also been elected to the following positions:

  • Co-opted Member (Peterborough City Council, Adults and Communities Scrutiny Committee)
  • Member (Peterborough City Council, Parish Liaison Working Group)
  • Member (Peterborough City Council, Tension Forum)
  • Independent Custody Visitor (Office of the Police and Crime Commissioner)

Following his work promoting the integration and cohesion of communities within Peterborough he was awarded a Peterborough City Council Civic Award for Community Involvement in 2020

David Ridgeway

David studied Botany at university and after graduating, spent a period working as a biology and science teacher at a Watford school. Following this he commenced training for ordination and has served in a number of parishes over past thirty years. Since 2015 he has been Rector of Castor with Upton, Stibbington and Water Newton; Marholm and Sutton. Before moving to the Peterborough area he spent 15 years in St Albans. Throughout his ministry David has tried to seek out ways in which the Church can serve the local community. In St Albans he was instrumental in setting up a Community Cafe and here in Peterborough helped to intiate the process of setting up the RNWP Good Neighbours Scheme..

Chris Wilde (Chair)

Chris spent his career in the automotive business, principally in Germany where he lived for many years. He was Treasurer and later Chair of the British Embassy School in Bad Godesberg which became the Independent Bonn International School after unification. He moved to Glinton in 2017 and is a member of Glinton Parish Council.
